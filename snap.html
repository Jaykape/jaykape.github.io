Chatting about photography

One thing that I realized that I've enjoyed so much is that I love to think about how a group of photos is presented.

In my early days, majority of photo sets I shot are not entirely tied together, meaning that removing some of them or rearranging them didnn't change their value. They were never intended to be arranged in the first place.

My later work will be much more involved in this aspect.


Dynamo

One of my frustrations is the lack of available single-hand film cameras that meet traditional SLR standards, such as interchangeable lenses and a hot shoe.

Most second-hand options are either broken or prone to breaking, and there are very few technicians available to repair them. In most cases, we have to wait a long time for repairs to be completed.

The goal of Dynamo project is to create an open-source, (most of the body) 3D-printable blueprint for a fully functional film camera that is easy to assemble, disassemble, and repair with replaceable parts.

While brands like Lomography offer plastic DIY cameras such as the Konstruktor SLR, these options have significant limitations, particularly when it comes to compatibility with lenses and flashes.
